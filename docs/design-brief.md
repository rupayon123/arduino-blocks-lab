# Arduino Blocks Lab Design Brief

## Creative Direction

Use a Classroom Circuit Bench direction: a friendly maker workbench for students learning Arduino with blocks, generated C++, wiring checks, and real upload. The product should feel hands-on, practical, and confident, like a classroom tool students can use without being talked down to.

## Brand Personality

- Playful, but still technically trustworthy.
- Bright Arduino blue with warm classroom accents.
- Clear enough for kids, credible enough for teachers.
- Human copy over startup copy.

## Avoid

- Purple-blue gradients.
- Glassmorphism cards.
- Floating dashboard mockups.
- Random glowing blobs.
- Generic feature grids.
- Startup-style hero sections.
- Vague corporate copy.
- Buzzwords like "unlock", "supercharge", "streamline", and "seamless".

## Create

- A distinct visual concept tied to Arduino, breadboards, wires, blocks, and upload flow.
- Strong typography with concise, specific copy.
- Purposeful spacing and stable responsive layout.
- A memorable hero section that signals the actual product immediately.
- A clear conversion path: start building, inspect code, check circuits, or open lessons.

## Default Design Process

Before a major website redesign, propose three creative directions, choose the strongest one for the project, and build from that direction. For Arduino Blocks Lab, the chosen direction is Classroom Circuit Bench.
